Self-supervised learning promises to remove the need for manual labeling, letting models scale to massive datasets and larger architectures. Unlike task-specific training, it can learn visual representations across diverse domains: from natural scenes to aerial images, using a single approach.

DINOv3 takes a big step toward this goal with a few key strategies.

ðŸ”´ First, it scales both the dataset and model size carefully, optimizing data preparation, architecture, and training.

ðŸ”´ Second, it introduces Gram anchoring, a method that prevents dense feature maps from degrading during long training schedules - a problem that has been hard to solve.

ðŸ”´ Third, post-hoc strategies improve flexibility in resolution, model size, and even alignment with text.

The result is a versatile vision foundation model that excels across many tasks without fine-tuning. DINOv3 produces high-quality dense features, outperforming previous self- and weakly-supervised models. The DINOv3 suite is designed to scale across different tasks, datasets, and deployment constraints, making it a strong baseline for modern computer vision applications.
